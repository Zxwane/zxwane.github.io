About Black Holes

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it.

Formation

Black holes form when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycle.

Types

There are three main types of black holes: stellar, supermassive, and intermediate.

Research

Scientists study black holes using various methods, including observations with telescopes and simulations.
